Our Story

Pastor Tim Keller, in his excellent book Every Good Endeavor, says, “…every good endeavor, even the simplest ones, pursued in response to God’s calling, can matter forever.” It is our hope at Church of the Holy Trinity that our endeavor to plant a church of Word and Sacrament in our neighborhoods east of Cleveland will matter deeply—for the good of our community, for as long as God calls us to this work.

Fr. Sean was called to plant this church after a period of discerning beginning in the autumn of 2014. After being away for nearly a decade, he is excited to be back in his hometown pursuing God’s call. While away studying for ministry, he always felt a gentle prod that God might call him back to Cleveland. Sean had been serving at the Church of the Holy Trinity in Pittsboro, NC—an Anglican church plant in semi-rural North Carolina. At this church plant, the community saw deep discipleship and spiritual friendships form. Sean wants to see the same happen in his hometown.

The Church of the Holy Trinity in Pittsboro, NC commissioned Sean and his family for this work after the Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes confirmed that there was a need for an Anglican church plant on the near east side of Cleveland. And there is a need—according to Barna Research Group, Cleveland ranked #38 in a list of America’s top Churchless Cities and #43 in America’s top Post-Christian Cities. More than this, many in the Cleveland area continue to struggle financially, are crippled by addiction (especially from the heroin epidemic sweeping parts of the country), or just simply lack deep connection to others around matters of utmost importance—including our connection to God.

Our vision for the near future is to begin gathering a core group of folks to form the launch team for the church plant—these may be people who have never before stepped a foot into a church to folks who can’t remember a day apart from the Christian faith. The only qualifications needed to be on the launch team are a commitment to:

  • Grow: Whether you’re new to the Christian faith or have been following Christ for more than half a century, we are looking for those who want to grow deep roots of faith, worshiping God with our hearts and our minds.
  • Come: Many of us have been part of groups in which people inconsistently show up. This makes it hard to establish trust and build momentum. Just “showing up” is crucial to beginning anything well.
  • Bring: One of the important characteristics we want to nurture in the culture of Holy Trinity is a spirit of hospitality. We need people who are willing to open the doors and welcome friends, neighbors, colleagues, and strangers to our gatherings.
  • Give: Would you be willing to invest in what God is doing through this church? Our life situations are all different, so our giving will look different, but there are three basic ways to give: time, talent, and treasure.


Our story as a Christian community is just now unfolding. Is God calling you to join us in this good work?
