Meet Our Pastor



We live in exciting and yet equally challenging times. Social media has connected us more than any other time in history and yet many of us feel more disconnected than we ever have. Culture changes rapidly and it’s hard to feel like we have any spiritual rootedness.

I know this spiritual discontentedness. I went through a period of spiritual wandering after my mother suddenly died when I was fourteen years old. Thanks be to God, the Church reached out to me and connected me to God and to Christian community. And this is nothing new—the great Church Father Augustine said over sixteen hundred years ago that “God has formed us for Himself and our hearts are restless until they find rest in Him.”

After discerning a call into ministry, I attended Cedarville University where I received a B.A. in Biblical Studies. It is also where I met my lovely wife Sonya. I then attended seminary and received a Masters of Divinity degree at Duke Divinity School.

While at Duke, I was blessed to serve at the Church of the Holy Trinity, an Anglican church plant in semi-rural Pittsboro, NC where I oversaw Discipleship and Catechesis ministries. In my time at the Church of the Holy Trinity, we saw deep community form and men and women become devoted disciples of Jesus Christ. The folks in Pittsboro commissioned me, Sonya, and our son Alasdair in June of 2015 to plant another Anglican church where I grew up on the eastside of Cleveland.

I’m excited to be an Anglican pastor in the 21st century. In the midst of the rapid change and disconnectedness of our time, I believe that Anglicanism has a rootedness in the ancient traditions and worship of the early church and yet an exciting future and eagerness to bring that rootedness to bear in our present moment. I have a passion for walking with folks—especially those who may be struggling or suffering—as they come to find rest in Jesus Christ and in Christian community.

I look forward to walking on this ancient-but-new road with you-



Rev. Sean A. Ewing
