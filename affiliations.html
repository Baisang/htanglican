Affiliations

Anglican Church in North America

The Church of the Holy Trinity is being planted as a congregation in The Anglican Church in North America. The Anglican Church in North America unites 112,000 Anglicans in nearly 1,000 congregations across the United States, Canada, and Mexico into a single Church. On April 16, 2009 it was recognized as a province of the global Anglican Communion, by the Primates of the Global Fellowship of Confessing Anglicans. The Most Rev. Dr. Foley Beach is the Archbishop of the Church.

Anglican Diocese of Christ our Hope

Fr. Sean was ordained as a deacon and then as a priest by the Rt. Rev. Steve Breedlove, presiding bishop of the Anglican Diocese of Christ our Hope. The mission of the Diocese of Christ our Hope is "to plant, build, and equip disciple-making Anglican churches, their people, and those who serve them." The Diocese of Christ our Hope is strongly committed to missional church planting and the development of next-generation leadership. Fr. Sean is excited to be planting a church in the Diocese of Christ our Hope as its unique charisms support the Church of the Holy Trinity.

Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes (ADGL)

The Church of the Holy Trinity is being planted in partnership with the Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes. The Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes includes nearly 50 parishes in Ohio, Michigan, Indiana, West Virginia, Kentucky, and Ontario, Canada. The Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes is committed to making disciples of Christ through church planting. The Rt. Rev. Ron Jackson currently serves as the Bishop of the Anglican Diocese of the Great Lakes.

Holy Trinity—Chatham

Fr. Sean served as a Curate over Discipleship and Catechesis ministries at Holy Trinity—Chatham after graduating from seminary. Holy Trinity—Chatham commissioned Fr. Sean and his family on Trinity Sunday of 2015 to plant Church of the Holy Trinity in Cleveland, OH. Holy Trinity—Chatham continues to support our church plant. The name of our church plant has been inspired by Holy Trinity—Chatham to signify the ongoing bond that we share.
